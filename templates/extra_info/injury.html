Injury Prevention/Treatment

Prevention

As previously mentioned, dynamic stretching before a workout along with static stretching after a workout will prevent future injury and contribute to flexibility. Give your body time to rest after an excercise. If you do have an injury tend to it carefully so it does not become a chronic injury. Drink plenty of water to prevent dehydration. If you have arthritis, or weakened joints, then stay away from high impact exercises such as running and jumping. Try to find low-impact exercises such as swimming and cycling.

Treatment

The to recovering from an injury is RICE. Not edible rice but an acronym containing essential information to succesfully recovering from an injury.

R - The 'R' stands for rest. Athletes develop somewhat of a tumultuous feeling from watching their teams battle while they sit on the sidelines. People often jump back into action too quickly which causes them to re-injure themselves.

I - The 'I' stands for ice. Ice can reduce swelling, bleeding, and inflammation.

C - The 'C' stands for compression which also prevents swelling.

E - The 'E' stands for elevation which also prevents swelling and inflammation.

Employing these techniques are actually very simple to comprehend. The hard part is to properly follow through with these principles. Your mindset is also very important. You should feel completely confident in your abilities after you recover. Depending on the severity of your injure your confidence. All in all, if you do happen to sustain an injury I hope you get better soon so you can get back to improving your physical capabilities.
